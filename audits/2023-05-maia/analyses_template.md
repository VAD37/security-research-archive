## What is an analysis?

An analysis is a written submission outlining:

- Your analysis of the codebase as a whole and any observations or advice you have about architecture, mechanism, or approach
- Broader concerns like systemic risks or centralization risks
- The approach you took in reviewing the code
- New insights and learnings from this audit

## Why submit an analysis?

Analyses are your best opportunity to provide the contest sponsor with valuable insights that go beyond any single vulnerability and extend to securing their codebase as a whole. Accordingly, the Analysis award pool is typically 7.5% of the total award pool.

In addition to financial incentives:
- Performance within advanced analyses will soon become a criteria for selection in an increasing number of opportunities, including ALL mitigation reviews, shortlist invitationals, and some other not-yet-announced offerings.
- Newer wardens submit 90% of spam and low-quality submissions and inherently are subject to greater scrutiny as a result. Choosing and providing a quality basic analysis provides greater motivation for lookouts/judges to spend more time reviewing the validity of your submissions.

### Awarding

Basic analyses are not eligible for the Analysis award pool (typically 5% of the total pool). However, submitting a quality basic-level analysis provides greater motivation for lookouts/judges to spend more time reviewing the validity of your submissions.

Advanced analyses graded A or B will be awarded from the Analysis award pool. The best advanced analysis gets a 30% bonus.
